1. Brand & Accent Colors (brand)
These are typically the primary glowing and utility colors of the simulator.

neon: The primary bright accent color (e.g., Cyan or Neon Green).

glow: The secondary shadow/glow accent color.

glass: The transparent overlay color used for visual effects.

yellow: The primary accent color for pulse/action buttons.

2. Main Backgrounds (background)
These define the largest background areas of your application's layout.

body: The main background color for the entire page.

terminal: The dedicated background color for the system log/terminal panel.

deepSlate: The darkest color used in gradients, typically at the edge of the screen.

3. LCD & Pixel States (lcd)
These define the specific colors of the simulated LCD hardware.

offPixel: The dark, unlit background color of the character segment (used in Cell.jsx).

onPixel: The bright, lit color of the character segment (for non-blinking pixels).

pulseOff: The background color used during the OFF cycle of the blinking animation.

pulseOn: The background color used during the ON cycle of the blinking animation.

screenBg: The background color of the inner LCD glass/panel, behind the dots (used in LcdScreen.jsx).

pcbFrame: The background color of the outer simulated PCB board.

pcbTrace: The color of the PCB traces (lines drawn on the board).

pinBg: The color of the circular backgrounds of the data pins.

mountHole: The color of the borders for the screen's mounting holes.

4. UI Components (ui)
These are specific colors for interaction and utility elements that require custom coloring.

rsOn: The color for the RS toggle switch when it is ON (Data Mode).

rwOn: The color for the RW toggle switch when it is ON (Read Mode).

enPulse: The color for the EN Pulse button (same as brand.yellow).

dataPinOn: The color of the data bus pins when they are HIGH (ON).

scrollTrack: The color of the terminal scrollbar track.

scrollThumb: The color of the terminal scrollbar thumb.
